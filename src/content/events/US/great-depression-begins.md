## Black Tuesday

In October 1929, the U.S. stock market crashed, marking the beginning of the Great Depression. On October 29, known as "Black Tuesday," the market experienced its worst day, with massive sell-offs and devastating losses.

## Economic Collapse

The crash triggered a cascade of economic failures:

- Widespread bank failures and loss of savings
- Unemployment reaching 25% at its peak
- Massive reduction in consumer spending
- International economic crisis
- Agricultural devastation, including the Dust Bowl

## Social Impact

The Depression profoundly affected American society:

- Millions lost their homes and jobs
- Widespread homelessness and poverty
- Migration of displaced workers seeking employment
- Growth of shanty towns called "Hoovervilles"
- Increased social tensions and labor unrest

## Political Response

The crisis led to significant political changes:

- Loss of faith in business and Republican leadership
- Election of Franklin D. Roosevelt in 1932
- Implementation of the New Deal programs
- Expansion of federal government power
- Shift in American political alignment

## Global Context

The Depression had worldwide effects and contributed to political instability that would enable the rise of extremist movements in Europe, including fascism in Germany and Italy.
